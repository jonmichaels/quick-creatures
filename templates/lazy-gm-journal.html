Building a Quick Monster

Use the Monster Statistics by Challenge Rating table to generate baseline stats for any CR foe. Then flavor them with narrative and optional mechanics.

Column Descriptions

  • Monster CR: Baseline difficulty.
  • Equivalent Character Level: Approximate level of a single character in a hard encounter vs. one monster of that CR.
  • AC/DC: Typical Armor Class and DC for abilities.
  • Hit Points: Baseline (range inside parentheses).
  • Proficient Ability Bonus: Attack bonus, proficient save/skill bonus (includes ability mod + proficiency).
  • Damage per Round: Total expected damage per round. For area effects that hit 2+ targets, use half.
  • Number of Attacks: Typical attacks per round.
  • Damage per Attack: Average damage and dice expression for each attack.

Building Steps

  1. Determine CR — compare to example monsters, or pick based on the party's level using the Equivalent Character Level column.
  2. Write down baseline stats from the table.
  3. Choose Proficient Abilities — use the Proficient Ability Bonus for any ability the monster should be good at (based on story).
  4. Determine Remaining Abilities — set non-proficient modifiers between −2 and +4 (default to +0).
  5. Optional: adjust AC, attacks, hit points, number of attacks, add damage types, monster features.

Quick Monster Statistics (even faster)

If you don't have the table handy, use these formulas (based on CR):

  • Armor Class = 12 + CR/2
  • Hit points = 15 × CR + 15
  • Proficient saves/skills = 4 + CR/2
  • Non-proficient = −2 to +2 (story-driven)
  • Attack bonus = 4 + CR/2
  • DC = 12 + CR/2
  • Total damage per round = 7 × CR + 5

Start with one attack. Add an extra attack at CR 2, 7, 11, 15. Split total damage across all attacks.

Note: Quick Creatures uses the full statistics table, not the quick formulas.

Monster Features

Damage Features

FeatureDescriptionEffect on Stats
Damaging Blast The creature has one or more single-target ranged attacks using the attack bonus and damage calculated above, dealing damage of an appropriate type. Uses existing attack/damage
Damage Reflection When hit by a melee attack within 5 feet, the attacker takes damage equal to half of one of this creature's attacks, of a type appropriate to the creature. −1 attack (already factored)
Damage Transference When the creature takes damage, it can transfer half or all of that damage to a willing creature within 30 or 60 feet. No change
Damaging Aura Each creature that starts its turn within 10 feet takes damage equal to half of one of this creature's attacks, of a type appropriate to the creature. −1 attack (already factored)
Damaging Burst As an action, the creature creates a burst in a 10-foot-radius sphere (around itself or at a point within 120 feet). Creatures in the area make a save; on a failure, they take damage equal to half the creature's total damage per round. On a success, half damage. Damage = half DPR; DC = chart ACDC
Energy Weapons The creature's weapon attacks deal extra damage of an appropriate type (energy, fire, cold, etc.). Can add on top of regular damage or replace some of it. +CR damage per attack

Movement & Control Features

FeatureDescriptionEffect on Stats
Cunning Action On each turn, the creature can use a bonus action to take the Dash, Disengage, or Hide action. No change
Knockdown When the creature hits with a melee attack, the target must succeed on a Strength saving throw or be knocked prone. DC = chart ACDC
Misty Step As a bonus action, the creature can teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space it can see. No change
Restraining Grab When the creature hits with a melee attack, the target is grappled (escape DC based on Strength or Dexterity). While grappled, the target is restrained. DC = chart ACDC
